How to read this. Each rider's line is kilometres ÷ days elapsed, projected to the finish on the assumption they hold that same average. It is a linear projection, not a forecast: nobody holds one pace for twenty days. It answers who has margin right now — not who will arrive.

The assumptions. Start 19 July 2026 at 20:00 CEST, cut-off 8 August at 23:59 CEST — 20 days and 4 hours (source: Lost Dot, who mark these details as provisional). The nominal route is roughly 4,800–5,000 km, but every rider plots their own line, so the slider exists to show how much the threshold depends on that guess. Current averages are depressed by the Norwegian opening and the westward detour to CP1 Flåm, so the group just below the line is less compromised than it looks. The four riders outside the General Classification are excluded.
