Every control point closes on its own deadline, so the line bends at each one: the CP1→CP2 leg demands the steepest pace of the whole race. Stay above the line and you make the next cut-off; drop below and you keep riding, but out of the General Classification.

How to read this

Each rider's line is kilometres ÷ days elapsed, projected forward at that same average. The black line is the deadline envelope: the distance you must have covered at each control's closing time, joined into one path. A rider is inside if their average still clears every remaining deadline — the binding one is whichever demands the most, shown in the tooltip. It is a linear projection, not a forecast: nobody holds one pace for twenty days. It answers who has margin right now, against the deadline that actually bites, not who will arrive.

The assumptions

Start 19 July 2026 at 20:00 CEST, cut-off 8 August at 23:59 CEST — 20 days and 4 hours (source: Lost Dot, who mark these details as provisional). The nominal route is roughly 4,800–5,000 km, but every rider plots their own line, so the slider exists to show how much the threshold depends on that guess. Current averages are depressed by the Norwegian opening and the westward detour to CP1 Flåm, so the group just below the line is less compromised than it looks.

The control points

Each closing time is taken from the organiser's configuration in the live tracker: CP1 Flåm 22 July 11:00, CP2 Tatra 30 July 11:00, CP3 Sarajevo 2 August 23:59, CP4 Leskovik 5 August 23:59, all CEST. Their distances along the route are estimated — CP1 anchored to the shortest distance any rider had covered when validating it (≈685 km), CP2–CP4 placed between that and the finish in proportion to the great-circle legs between the checkpoints. So the exact bends are approximate, but the shape is real: the CP1→CP2 leg is by far the steepest, needing about 280 km/day, well above the race-long average of 245. Under the Lost Dot rules, qualifying for the General Classification requires validating at every control within its window; miss one and you forfeit classification and your official race time, though you may ride on. Those riders are drawn as a calavera rather than a dot — still riding, no longer classified — and for them the line only tracks reaching Kalamata by 8 August. The four riders the organiser listed as outside the classification from the start are excluded entirely.
